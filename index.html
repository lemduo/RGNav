RGNav: A Risk-Guided Method for UGV Navigation in Dynamic Environments

Abstract

Autonomous navigation of unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs) in dynamic environments is a challenging task that requires them to perceive current local risks and account for long-term risks simultaneously. This letter proposes RGNav, a risk-guided policy for UGV navigation. RGNav incorporates a region risk module that extracts risk cues from consecutive observations and integrates them with scene context through attention, enabling the UGV to identify critical risk regions. A risk-guided reward is also designed based on long-term risk estimated from the return distribution under the teacher policy, guiding the UGV to avoid high-risk states. Extensive experiments demonstrate that RGNav outperforms mainstream methods in both safety and efficiency. We also deploy RGNav on a real UGV and show that it achieves reliable performance in real-world environments.
